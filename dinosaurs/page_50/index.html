491. Talenkauen

Brief Description: A rare ornithopod from South America.

Additional Information: Description of Talenkauen. Resembling the Dryosaurus in shape and build, Talenkauen stood out with a proportionally longer neck, a feature that might have been advantageous in foraging for vegetation. This dinosaur was an ornithopod, a group f ornithischian known for their bipedal stance, suggesting that it moved primarily on two legs.

492. Talos

Brief Description: - This dinosaur was found with an injured big toe.

Additional Information: Restoration. Talos is a troodontid, a group of small, bird-like, gracile maniraptorans.All troodontids have many unique features of the skull, such as closely spaced teeth in the lower jaw, and large numbers of teeth. Troodontids have sickle-claws and raptorial hands, and some of the highest non-avian encephalization quotients, meaning they were behaviourally advanced and had keen senses. [2]

493. Tangvayosaurus

Brief Description: - This Laotian titanosaur was closely related to Phuwiangosaurus.

Additional Information: Tangvayosaurus (meaning “Tang Vay lizard”) is a genus of sauropod dinosaur from the Aptian-Albian age Lower Cretaceous Gres superieurs Formation of Savannakhet Province, Laos. It was a basal somphospondylan , about 15 m long, and is known from the remains of two or three individuals.

494. Tanius

Brief Description: - Not much is known about this Chinese hadrosaur.

Additional Information: The name Tanius means “Tan’s beast” in honor of Tan Xichou, a prominent Chinese paleontologist who made many contributions to the study of dinosaurs in China. Tanius was a small to medium-sized theropod, measuring around 16-20 feet (5-6 meters) in length and weighing around 440-880 pounds (200-400 kilograms).

495. Tanycolagreus

Brief Description: - This mysterious theropod was once thought to be a species of Coelurus.

Additional Information: Physical Description. Tanycolagreus was a remarkable theropod dinosaur with unique features and a notable size. Its physical characteristics give insight into its lifestyle and hunting abilities. Size and Build. Tanycolagreus measured around 3.3 meters (approximately 11 feet) in length as a young adult.
