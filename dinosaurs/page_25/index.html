241. Huaxiagnathus

Brief Description: - One of the biggest dino-birds of its time.

Additional Information: Huaxiagnathus is an extinct genus of coelurosaurian theropod dinosaur from the Lower Cretaceous of China. Huaxiagnathus was larger than the related Sinosauropteryx, with the largest specimen about 1.8 meters (5.9 feet) in length. [1]The name Huaxiagnathus is derived from the Chinese Hua Xia, 華夏, a traditional word for “China”, and from the Greek gnathos, Latinised into gnathus, meaning “jaw.”

243. Huayangosaurus

Brief Description: - Could this have been the ancestor of all the stegosaurs?

Additional Information: Huayangosaurus is a genus of stegosaurian dinosaur from the Middle Jurassic of China.The name derives from “Huayang” (華陽), an alternate name for Sichuan (the province where it was discovered), and “saurus”, meaning “lizard”. It lived during the Bathonian to Callovian stages, around 165 million years ago, some 20 million years before its famous relative, Stegosaurus appeared in North America.

246. Huxleysaurus

Brief Description: - Named after the famous biologist Thomas Henry Huxley.

Additional Information: Originally classified as a species of Iguanodon, this genus stands out in the evolution of dinosaurs for its unique anatomical features. Recognized by paleontologists for its relevance to dinosaur lineage and evolutionary science, Huxleysaurus offers insight into ancient ecosystems and the biodiversity of prehistoric times.

247. Hypselosaurus

Brief Description: - This titanosaur’s eggs were a foot in diameter.

Additional Information: Hypselosaurus Facts Hypselosaurus, also known as the “highest lizard,” was a genus of herbivorous dinosaur that lived during the Late Cretaceous period, around 70 million years ago. It was a member of the group of long-necked dinosaurs known as sauropods, which were some of the largest animals to have ever lived on land.

248. Hypselospinus

Brief Description: - It was once classified as a species of Iguanodon.

Additional Information: Physical Description. Hypselospinus was a mid-sized dinosaur that could reach lengths of about 6 meters (19.7 feet). It had a robust body, with adaptations suitable for a herbivorous diet. Its teeth were specifically designed for grinding plant material, showing that it relied heavily on vegetation for food.
