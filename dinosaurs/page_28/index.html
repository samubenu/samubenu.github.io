273. Khaan

Brief Description: - Few small mammals dared face the wrath of this dinosaur.

Additional Information: The type species Khaan mckennai was named by James M. Clark et al. in 2001. The genus name is derived from Mongol khaan, “lord” or “ruler”.The specific name honours the paleontologist Malcolm Carnegie McKenna.. The holotype IGM 100/1127 consists of an almost complete skeleton found together with another specimen, IGM 100/1002. Together they were informally referred to as “Romeo and Juliet”.

276. Kol

Brief Description: - It’s tied with Mei for “shortest dinosaur name.”

Additional Information: Kol (from the Mongolian хөл/köl, meaning “foot”) is an extinct genus of coelurosaurian theropod dinosaur from the Late Cretaceous of Mongolia.The type and only species is Kol ghuva (from the Mongolian гува/ghuv-a, meaning “beautiful”).The type specimen was excavated from the Ukhaa Tolgod locality of the Djadochta Formation, dating to about 75 million years ago.

278. Kosmoceratops

Brief Description: - This ceratopsian had a bizarre, downward-folding frill.

Additional Information: Kosmoceratops (/ ˌ k ɒ z m ə ˈ s ɛ r ə t ɒ p s / [1]) is a genus of ceratopsid dinosaur that lived in North America about 76-75.9 million years ago during the Late Cretaceous period.Specimens were discovered in Utah in the Kaiparowits Formation of the Grand Staircase-Escalante National Monument in 2006 and 2007, including an adult skull and postcranial skeleton and partial subadults.

279. Kotasaurus

Brief Description: - One of the few sauropods to be discovered in India.

Additional Information: Kotasaurus is a genus of sauropod dinosaur that lived during the Early Jurassic period, around 180 million years ago. It was named after the Kota Formation in India, where its fossils were first discovered. Kotasaurus was a relatively small sauropod, measuring around 10 meters (33 feet) in length and likely weighing around 2-3 metric tons.

280. Kritosaurus

Brief Description: - A famous, but poorly understood hadrosaur.

Additional Information: Kritosaurus Facts Kritosaurus is a genus of hadrosaurid dinosaur that lived during the Late Cretaceous period, around 74-67 million years ago. It is known from fossils found in North America, including the states of Montana, South Dakota, and Wyoming.
