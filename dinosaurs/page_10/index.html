92. Brachyceratops

Brief Description: - A little-known ceratopsian from North America.

Additional Information: Brachyceratops (‘short horned face’) is a dubious genus of ceratopsian dinosaur known only from partial juvenile specimens dating to the late Cretaceous Period of Montana, United States. Brachyceratops has historically been known from juvenile remains, with one specimen having since been re-classified as Rubeosaurus ovatus .

96. Buitreraptor

Brief Description: - The oldest raptor ever discovered in South America.

Additional Information: Buitreraptor, also known as the “Vulture Raider”, was a species of theropod dinosaur that lived during the early Cretaceous period, approximately 125 million years ago. It was a small to medium-sized dinosaur, measuring about 6 meters in length and weighing between 50 and 100 kg.

100. Carnotaurus

Brief Description: - The shortest arms of any meat-eating dinosaur with horns to match.

Additional Information: Carnotaurus (/ ˌ k ɑːr n oʊ ˈ t ɔːr ə s /; lit. ‘ meat bull ‘) is a genus of theropod dinosaur that lived in South America during the Late Cretaceous period, probably sometime between 72 and 69 million years ago. The only species is Carnotaurus sastrei.Known from a single well-preserved skeleton, it is one of the best-understood theropods from the Southern Hemisphere.
