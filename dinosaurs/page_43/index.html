Dinosaur Encyclopedia - Page 43/50

421. Spinosaurus

  • This dinosaur was distinguished by the sail-like structure on its back.

422. Spinostropheus

  • This theropod was once thought to be a species of Elaphrosaurus.

423. Stegoceras

  • This small herbivore was built for high-speed head-butting.

424. Struthiomimus

  • This “ostrich mimic” roamed the plains of North America.

425. Struthiosaurus

  • The smallest nodosaur yet discovered.

426. Stygimoloch

  • Its name means “demon from the river of death.” Got your attention yet?

427. Styracosaurus

  • Winner of the “most elaborate head display” competition.

428. Suchomimus

  • A fish-eating dinosaur with a distinct crocodilian profile.

429. Sulaimanisaurus

  • One of the few dinosaurs ever to be discovered in Pakistan.

430. Supersaurus

  • No, it didn’t wear a cape, but this giant dino was still impressive.
