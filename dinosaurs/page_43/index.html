421. Prosaurolophus

Brief Description: - The likely ancestor of both Saurolophus and Parasaurolophus.

Additional Information: Prosaurolophus (/ ˌ p r oʊ s ɔː ˈ r ɒ l ə f ə s /; meaning “before Saurolophus”, in comparison to the later dinosaur with a similar head crest) is a genus of hadrosaurid (or duck-billed) dinosaur from the Late Cretaceous of North America.It is known from the remains of at least 25 individuals belonging to two species, including skulls and skeletons, but it remains obscure.

427. Pyroraptor

Brief Description: - This “fire thief” prowled the plains of prehistoric France.

Additional Information: Pyroraptor (meaning “fire thief”) is an extinct genus of paravian dinosaur, probably a dromaeosaurid or unenlagiid, from the Late Cretaceous Ibero-Armorican island, of what is now southern France.It lived during the late Campanian and early Maastrichtian stages, approximately 72 million years ago.It is known from a single partial specimen that was found in Provence in 1992, after a forest fire.

429. Qianzhousaurus

Brief Description: - This long-snouted tyrannosaur has been nicknamed Pinocchio Rex.

Additional Information: Qianzhousaurus (meaning “Qianzhou lizard”) is a genus of tyrannosaurid dinosaur that lived in Asia during the Maastrichtian age of the Late Cretaceous period. There is currently only one species named, the type species Qianzhousaurus sinensis, which is a member of the tribe Alioramini and most closely related to Alioramus, the only other known alioramin.
