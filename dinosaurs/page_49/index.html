483. Stygimoloch

Brief Description: - Its name means “demon from the river of death.” Got your attention yet?

Additional Information: Facts 1: The Stygimoloch is a dinosaur that belongs to the family Pachycephalosauridae. The Stygimoloch was a herbivorous dinosaur that lived during the Late Cretaceous period, approximately 76 to 75 million years ago. It is known for its unique skull structure. Facts 2: The name “Stygimoloch” means “devil from the river of the underworld”.

485. Suchomimus

Brief Description: - A fish-eating dinosaur with a distinct crocodilian profile.

Additional Information: Suchomimus (meaning “crocodile mimic”) is a genus of spinosaurid dinosaur that lived between 125 and 112 million years ago in what is now Niger, North Africa, during the Aptian to early Albian stages of the Early Cretaceous Period.It was named and described by paleontologist Paul Sereno and colleagues in 1998, based on a partial skeleton from the Elrhaz Formation.

490. Talarurus

Brief Description: - This ankylosaur was discovered in the Gobi Desert.

Additional Information: Talarurus (/ ˌ t æ l ə ˈ r ʊər ə s / TAL-ə-ROOR-əs; meaning “basket tail” or “wicker tail”) is a genus of ankylosaurid dinosaur that lived in Asia during the Late Cretaceous period, about 96 million to 89 million years ago. The first remains of Talarurus were discovered in 1948 and later described by the Russian paleontologist Evgeny Maleev with the type species T. plicatospineus.
