162. Edmontosaurus

Brief Description: - This large, duck-billed herbivore was a contemporary of T. Rex.

Additional Information: Edmontosaurus (/ ɛ d ˌ m ɒ n t ə ˈ s ɔːr ə s / ed-MON-tə-SOR-əs) (meaning “lizard from Edmonton”), with the second species often colloquially and historically known as Anatosaurus or Anatotitan (meaning “duck lizard” and “giant duck”), is a genus of hadrosaurid (duck-billed) dinosaur.It contains two known species: Edmontosaurus regalis and Edmontosaurus annectens.

Brief Description: - This Transylvanian dinosaur is almost as controversial as Dracula.

Additional Information: Elopteryx is a genus of paravian theropod dinosaur based on fragmentary fossils found in Late Cretaceous rocks of Romania.The single species, Elopteryx nopcsai, was known only from very incomplete material until new specimens were reported in the 21st century. 169. Elrhazosaurus

Brief Description: - Once classified as a species of Valdosaurus.

Additional Information: Elrhazosaurus is based on MNHN GDF 332, a left thigh bone collected by Philippe Taquet from the upper part of the Elrhaz Formation of Gadoufaoua in the Ténéré Desert, Agadez, Niger.This rock unit dates to the late Aptian stage, approximately 115 million years ago. The bone was designated as the type specimen of a new species of Valdosaurus (V. nigeriensis) by Peter Galton and Taquet in 1982.
