171. Eoabelisaurus

Brief Description: - The earliest abelisaurid theropod yet identified.

Additional Information: Eoabelisaurus (/ ˈ i oʊ ə ˌ b ɛ l ɪ ˈ s ɔː r ə s /) is a genus of abelisauroid theropod dinosaur from the Lower Jurassic Cañadón Asfalto Formation of the Cañadón Asfalto Basin in Argentina, South America.The generic name combines a Greek ἠώς, (eos), “dawn”, with the name Abelisaurus, in reference to the fact it represents an early relative of the latter.

172. Eobrontosaurus

Brief Description: - This “dawn brontosaurus” isn’t accepted by most experts.

Additional Information: Eobrontosaurus was a massive sauropod dinosaur known for its impressive size and unique anatomical features. Its physical characteristics allowed it to thrive in the habitats of the Late Jurassic period. Size and Scale. Eobrontosaurus reached an estimated length of about 21 meters, which is approximately 69 feet.

173. Eocarcharia

Brief Description: - This “dawn shark” prowled the woodlands of northern Africa.

Additional Information: Maxilla. Eocarcharia (meaning “dawn shark”) is a genus of allosauroid theropod dinosaur from the Early Cretaceous Elrhaz Formation that lived in the Sahara 112 million years ago, in what today is the country of Niger.It was discovered in 2000 on an expedition led by University of Chicago paleontologist Paul Sereno.The type and only species is Eocarcharia dinops. 178. Eotriceratops

Brief Description: - This “dawn Triceratops” was recently discovered in Canada.

Additional Information: Eotriceratops was in 2007 placed in the Chasmosaurinae.In a cladistic analysis, it was recovered as a close relative of Triceratops, Nedoceratops and Torosaurus.It would have been the sister species of Triceratops.In view of its greater age, the describing authors considered it more likely that Eotriceratops was in fact basal to, lower in the evolutionary tree than, the other three genera.
