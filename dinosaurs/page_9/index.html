82. Batyrosaurus

Brief Description: - One of the most basal hadrosaurs yet identified.

Additional Information: Belonging to the group of Ornithopoda, this dinosaur is a member of the Hadrosauroidae. Its type species, Batyrosaurus rozhdestvenskyi, is the only species and the name refers to the Russian paleontologist A. N. Rozhdestvensky. The timeline of this dino stretches from the Santonian to the Early Campanian (Late Cretaceous), pinpointing its existence to between 86.3 and 70.6 million years ago.

86. Bellusaurus

Brief Description: - A herd of this sauropod drowned in a flash flood.

Additional Information: Bellusaurus was a sauropodomorph dinosaur, a group of dinosaurs that are characterized by their large size and long necks. Based on the fossils that have been discovered, scientists estimate that Bellusaurus was about 20 feet in length and weighed around 2-3 tons.

87. Berberosaurus

Brief Description: - This “Berber lizard” has proven difficult to classify.

Additional Information: Berberosaurus (meaning “Berber lizard”, in reference to the Berbers of Morocco) is a genus of neotheropod dinosaur, possibly a ceratosaur, from the Toarcian-age (Lower Jurassic) “Toundoute Continental Series” (Azilal Formation) found in the Central High Atlas of Toundoute, Ouarzazate, Morocco.The type species of the genus Berberosaurus is B. liassicus, in reference to the Lias epoch.

89. Borogovia

Brief Description: - This theropod was named after a Lewis Carroll poem.

Additional Information: Borogovia is classified as a theropod dinosaur, which means it was a meat-eating dinosaur. Although its classification is still debated, some researchers believe that it was a member of the Oviraptorosauria group of theropods, which were known for their bird-like features and toothless beaks.
