463. Siamodon

Brief Description: - This ornithopod was recently discovered in Thailand.

Additional Information: Siamodon is known from the holotype PRC-4, a well-preserved left maxilla and from the referred materials PRC-5, an isolated maxillary tooth and PRC-6, a braincase.It was collected in the Ban Saphan Hin site, Nakhon Ratchasima Province, from the Khok Kruat Formation, dating to the Aptian stage of the late Early Cretaceous, about 125-113 million years ago.

467. Sinocalliopteryx

Brief Description: - The biggest “dino-bird” yet discovered.

Additional Information: Sinocalliopteryx (meaning ‘Chinese beautiful feather’) is a genus of carnivorous coelurosaurian theropod dinosaurs from the Lower Cretaceous Yixian Formation of China (Jianshangou Beds, dating to 124.6 Ma).. While similar to the related Huaxiagnathus, Sinocalliopteryx were larger. The type specimen, at 2.37 meters (7.78 ft) in length, in 2007 was the largest known compsognathid exemplar.

468. Sinornithoides

Brief Description: - A small, feathered dinosaur closely related to Troodon.

Additional Information: Sinornithoides (meaning “Chinese bird form”) is a genus of troodontid theropod dinosaurs containing the single species Sinornithoides youngi. S. youngi lived during the Early Cretaceous (Aptian/Albian stage, around 113 million years ago). [1] It measured approximately one meter long (3.3 ft).It lived in Inner Mongolia, China, and probably ate invertebrates and other small prey.
