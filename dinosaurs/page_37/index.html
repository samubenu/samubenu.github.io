361. Piatnitzkysaurus

  • Its teeth were as sharp as its name is funny.

362. Pisanosaurus

  • One of the earliest known ornithischian dinosaurs.

363. Piveteausaurus

  • No one is quite sure what to make of this theropod dinosaur.

364. Planicoxa

  • A medium-sized iguanodont of early Cretaceous North America.

365. Plateosaurus

  • This herd dinosaur blackened the plains of the late Triassic.

366. Pneumatoraptor

  • This “air thief” was recently discovered in Hungary.

367. Poekilopleuron

  • It may (or may not) have been a species of Megalosaurus.

368. Polacanthus

  • An extremely spiky ankylosaur of the middle Cretaceous.

369. Prenoceratops

  • A close relative of Leptoceratops.

370. Proa

  • This ornithopod was named after its prow-shaped jaw.
