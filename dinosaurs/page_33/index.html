323. Malawisaurus

Brief Description: - The first titanosaur to be found with an intact skull.

Additional Information: Malawisaurus Facts Malawisaurus is a genus of titanosaurian sauropod dinosaur that lived during the Early Cretaceous period, around 110 to 100 million years ago, in what is now Africa. Its name means “Malawi lizard”, in reference to the country where its fossils were first discovered.

326. Mantellodon

Brief Description: - This Iguanodon refugee may or may not deserve its own genus.

Additional Information: Mantellodon was a fascinating dinosaur with unique traits that set it apart from others. Its size and distinctive features provide insight into how it adapted to its environment during the Cretaceous period. Size and Scale. 328. Marshosaurus

Brief Description: - Named after the famous paleontologist Othniel C. Marsh.

Additional Information: Marshosaurus Facts Marshosaurus was a genus of theropod dinosaur that lived in North America during the Late Jurassic period, approximately 155 to 145 million years ago. It was a small to medium-sized predator, with adults estimated to have been around 4 to 5 meters (13 to 16 feet) long and weighing up to 150 kg (330 lbs).
