Dinosaur Encyclopedia - Page 29/50

281. Magnirostris

  • This ceratopsian had an unusually big beak.

282. Magnosaurus

  • Once thought to be a species of Megalosaurus.

283. Magyarosaurus

  • This dwarf titanosaur was probably confined to a small island.

284. Mahakala

  • This dino-bird was named after a Buddhist deity.

285. Maiasaura

  • This “good mother lizard” kept close tabs on her young.

286. Majungasaurus

  • Fairly–or unfairly–known as the “cannibal dinosaur.”

287. Malawisaurus

  • The first titanosaur to be found with an intact skull.

288. Mamenchisaurus

  • The longest-necked dinosaur that ever lived.

289. Manidens

  • A strangely toothed relative of Heterodontosaurus.

290. Mantellodon

  • This Iguanodon refugee may or may not deserve its own genus.
