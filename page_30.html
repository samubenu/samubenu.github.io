Dinosaur Encyclopedia - Page 30/50

291. Mapusaurus

  • This huge carnivore was closely related to Giganotosaurus.

292. Marshosaurus

  • Named after the famous paleontologist Othniel C. Marsh.

293. Massospondylus

  • This small, lithe, bipedal plant-eater roamed the plains of South Africa.

294. Maxakalisaurus

  • One of the biggest titanosaurs ever found in Brazil.

295. Megalosaurus

  • The first dinosaur ever to be discovered and named.

296. Megapnosaurus

  • Its name is Greek for “big dead lizard.”

297. Megaraptor

  • Despite its name, it wasn’t really a raptor.

298. Melanorosaurus

  • Probably the largest prosauropod that ever lived.

299. Mendozasaurus

  • This titanosaur was ancestral to Futalognkosaurus.

300. Mercuriceratops

  • This ceratopsian was discovered on the U.S./Canada border.
