Dinosaur Encyclopedia - Page 39/50

381. Qiupalong

  • This “bird mimic” dinosaur was recently discovered in China.

382. Quaesitosaurus

  • This titanosaur may have had remarkably sharp hearing.

383. Rahiolisaurus

  • This Indian dinosaur is represented by seven tangled individuals.

384. Rajasaurus

  • This “prince lizard” lived in what is now modern-day India.

385. Raptorex

  • A pint-sized precursor of T. Rex.

386. Rebbachisaurus

  • A poorly understood sauropod from northern Africa.

387. Rinchenia

  • Named after the famous paleontologist Rinchen Barsbold.

388. Rinconsaurus

  • A modestly sized titanosaur of South America.

389. Riojasaurus

  • One of the few prosauropods known to have lived in South America.

390. Sahaliyania

  • This hadrosaur’s name is Manchurian for “black.”
