Dinosaur Encyclopedia - Page 36/50

351. Paralititan

  • This huge sauropod was discovered recently in Egypt.

352. Paranthodon

  • This stegosaur was discovered over 150 years ago.

353. Pararhabdodon

  • The Western European equivalent of Tsintaosaurus.

354. Parasaurolophus

  • Possibly the loudest dinosaur ever to roam the earth.

355. Pedopenna

  • One of the earliest known dino-birds.

356. Pegomastax

  • This dinosaur was covered with porcupine-like bristles.

357. Peloroplites

  • This “monstrous Hoplite” was recently discovered in Utah.

358. Pentaceratops

  • This “five-horned” herbivore really had only three.

359. Philovenator

  • As its name says this dinosaur “loved to hunt.”

360. Phuwiangosaurus

  • This titanosaur was discovered in modern-day Thailand.
